## Technical Details

The fp8 K-cache configuration changes the numeric behavior in a way that breaks greedy speculative decoding for Model A. The simple run-gen gate (single-pass argmax) passes, but the iterative spec gate (repeated K-cache reads across multiple speculation rounds) exposes numeric drift.

This suggests:
1. fp8 quantization of K-cache may accumulate error across multiple reads
2. The 9B model may be more sensitive to K-cache precision than the 27B models
3. OR the st-modelopt variant has different numeric characteristics

The blocker is not performance (timing was essentially flat to slightly slower) — it's correctness.

## Recommendation

**DO NOT FLIP** `BW24_KV_K=fp8` to default until Model A self-consistency is resolved. Options:
1. Investigate why fp8 K-cache breaks spec for Model A (quantization scheme, accumulation, precision)
2. Consider model-specific K-cache format override
3. Require stricter gate (run-spec) before any K-cache format flip
