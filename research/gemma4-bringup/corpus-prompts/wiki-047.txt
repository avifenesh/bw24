--- title: White-Box Routing type: concept tags: [routing, inference, hidden-state, moe, speculative-decoding, internal-signals] sources: [] updated: 2026-06-29 --- # White-Box Routing **White-box routing** is the umbrella idea — recurring across several systems in this wiki — that serving and control decisions should be made by *reading a model's own internal signals* rather than by treating the model as an opaque box that only emits final tokens. The signals are the things a forward pass already produces but normally discards: per-layer hidden states, attention scores, raw logit distributions, router gate weights, and aggregate token-frequency statistics. A *black-box* router decides where to send a request, how long to draft, or which expert to keep using only externally observable features (the prompt text, request length, a learned predictor over the input). A *white-box* router instead peeks inside the live computation and routes on what the model is actually doing.
