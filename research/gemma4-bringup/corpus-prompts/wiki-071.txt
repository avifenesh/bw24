--- title: Functional Subspace Collapse type: concept tags: [moe, expert-merge, expert-pruning, model-compression, routing, reap] sources: [shared-delta-moe-project, gemma-expert-atlas] updated: 2026-06-29 --- # Functional Subspace Collapse Functional subspace collapse is the failure mode that makes expert *merging* worse than expert *pruning* for compressing Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) models on generative tasks. The term comes from Cerebras's REAP work (paper arxiv 2510.13999, blog and code released late 2025).[^1][^2] The intuition: a healthy MoE router does **input-dependent mixing** — for one token it might blend 70% of expert A with 30% of expert B, then shift to 40/60 on the next token. Current merge techniques (M-SMoE, HC-SMoE) replace A and B with a single static average and combine their router gate-values by summation. The router loses its freedom to choose; it is forced to apply that one fixed average to all inputs.
