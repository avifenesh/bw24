--- title: Abstract Interpretation and Soundness type: concept tags: [static-analysis, program-analysis, formal-methods, abstract-interpretation, soundness] sources: [] updated: 2026-06-29 --- # Abstract Interpretation and Soundness **Abstract interpretation** is a theory of sound approximation of program semantics, formalized by Patrick and Radhia Cousot in two foundational POPL papers (1977 and 1979).[^1] The idea: instead of running a program over its exact (concrete) values — which is undecidable in general by Rice's theorem and the halting problem — you run it over an **abstract domain** that keeps only the property you care about. Concrete sets of states are mapped into abstract elements by an *abstraction function* α; abstract elements map back to the sets they represent by a *concretization function* γ. The domains are ordered sets, usually lattices, and program operations are modeled by monotonic *abstract transfer functions*.
