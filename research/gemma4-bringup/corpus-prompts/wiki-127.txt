--- title: Attention Sinks type: concept tags: [attention, kv-cache, long-context, softmax, quantization, streaming, sparse-attention] sources: [] updated: 2026-06-29 --- # Attention Sinks An **attention sink** is a position (or a synthetic logit) that absorbs a large, semantically meaningless share of a Transformer's attention mass. The phenomenon was named and explained by Xiao et al. in **StreamingLLM** (arXiv 2309.17453, ICLR 2024): when visualizing Llama-2's attention, they found that across most layers heads dump heavy attention onto the very first tokens regardless of what is being predicted, behaving like a graph-theory *sink node* that receives flow but passes none on.[^1] The root cause is the softmax normalization: attention weights must sum to exactly 1.0, so a head with nothing relevant to attend to still has to put its "budget" somewhere — Evan Miller called this a "deafening democracy where abstention is disallowed."[^1] Initial tokens get a small baseline bias (they appear in every training sample) that training amplifies into a dedicated dumping ground. The same pattern had been seen earlier in BERT (heavy attention on `[SEP]` and periods, used as a no-op)
