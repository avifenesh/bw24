--- title: Transformer Engine type: entity status: active tags: [nvidia, library, fp8, nvfp4, mxfp8, mixed-precision, training, blackwell, cuda] sources: [research-2026-06-20-aiml-fp4-microscaling-nvfp4-mxfp4, qwen36-27b-server-config] updated: 2026-06-29 --- # Transformer Engine Transformer Engine (TE) is NVIDIA's open-source library for accelerating Transformer models on NVIDIA GPUs with low-precision (sub-FP16) arithmetic. It provides a collection of highly optimized building blocks — fused Transformer layers, attention, GEMM kernels — plus an automatic-mixed-precision-style autocast API that wraps framework code, internally maintaining the scaling factors and amax history that low-precision training needs.[^1] TE exposes a PyTorch and a JAX/Flax frontend over a framework-agnostic C++/CUDA core, so the same kernels can be integrated by other deep-learning libraries. It supports FP8 (E4M3/E5M2) on Hopper, Ada, and Blackwell, and adds the two Blackwell-native microscaling formats — MXFP8 and NVFP4 — on Blackwell-class silicon.[^1] In this wiki TE matters as the **reference implementation** for the NVFP4 numerical recipe: the precise scaling formulas, the Random Hadamard Transform placement, and the 2D weight scaling described on [[fp4-microscaling]] are pinned to TE's spec and flags, not to a paper alone.
