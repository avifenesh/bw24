--- title: Quantization-Aware Training (QAT) type: concept tags: [quantization, qat, fake-quant, straight-through-estimator, fp4, nvfp4, accuracy-recovery, training] sources: [research-2026-06-20-aiml-fp4-microscaling-nvfp4-mxfp4] updated: 2026-06-29 --- # Quantization-Aware Training (QAT) Quantization-aware training is the middle rung of the PTQ → QAT → QAD accuracy-recovery ladder: a short fine-tuning phase, run *after* a quantization recipe has been applied, that teaches the higher-precision weights and activations to live inside a low-precision format's representable grid.[^1] Unlike [[post-training-quantization]] (PTQ), which only calibrates scales against a held-out dataset and never updates weights, QAT injects a training loop in which the model sees its own rounding and clipping errors and adapts to them — yielding a smoother, lower-loss transition into the target format.[^1] It is the workhorse for [[fp4-microscaling|FP4]] accuracy recovery on small and mid-size models, where the FP4 PTQ drop is non-negligible and the tiny block-16 of [[nvfp4-production|NVFP4]] neutralizes many traditional outlier-mitigation tricks.[^1] The mechanism is **fake quantization** plus the **straight-through estimator (STE)** — both inherited from the foundational integer-quantization work of Jacob et al.
