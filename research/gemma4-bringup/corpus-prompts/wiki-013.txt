--- title: Gradient Noise Threshold type: concept tags: [quantization, fp4, nvfp4, pretraining, training-stability, stochastic-rounding] sources: [] updated: 2026-06-30 --- # Gradient Noise Threshold The gradient noise threshold is a quantitative rule for when low-precision (4-bit) training stops paying off, introduced in *FP4 All the Way: Fully Quantized Training of LLMs* (Chmiel, Fishman, Banner, Soudry — Intel / NVIDIA-Israel / Technion).[^1] The rule: training begins to degrade once the **gradient norm falls below roughly √3 times the quantization noise** introduced by the 4-bit format.[^1] While the signal (the true gradient) is large relative to the rounding error, stochastic rounding averages the per-step quantization noise out across a batch and the optimization trajectory is essentially unaffected. Once the gradient norm shrinks past this threshold, the per-element quantization noise stops being averaged away and starts dominating the update direction, so the quantized gradient no longer points reliably downhill. This regime is reached in practice during the **final learning-rate decay phase** of a long pretraining run, when gradient magnitudes naturally collapse.
