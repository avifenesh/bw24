--- title: Hazard Pointers type: concept tags: [lock-free, concurrency, memory-reclamation, synchronization, aba-problem, cpp] sources: [research-2026-07-09-systems-smr] updated: 2026-07-10 --- # Hazard Pointers Hazard pointers are a safe-memory-reclamation (SMR) technique for lock-free data structures, introduced by Maged M. Michael (IBM) in the 2004 IEEE TPDS paper "Hazard Pointers: Safe Memory Reclamation for Lock-Free Objects."[^1] They solve the core problem of lock-free programming: when one thread removes a node from a shared structure, another thread may still hold a pointer into that node, so freeing it immediately risks a use-after-free. The mechanism gives each thread a small fixed number of single-writer/multi-reader **hazard pointer** slots. Before dereferencing a shared pointer, a reader publishes it into one of its slots (a store that other threads can observe) and then re-validates that the pointer is still reachable. A node that is continuously protected by some thread's hazard pointer from a moment before its removal can never be reclaimed while that protection holds.[^2] Reclamation is therefore a two-phase, deferred operation.
