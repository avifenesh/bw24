--- title: pycapacity type: entity tags: [robotics, biomechanics, kinematics, polytopes, python] sources: [] updated: 2026-06-29 --- `pycapacity` is an open-source Python package for real-time evaluation of task-space physical-ability metrics ("capacity") of robots and human musculoskeletal models, built on polytopes and ellipsoids. It is developed by the Auctus team (Inria) — Antun Skuric, Vincent Padois, and David Daney — and described in a resume paper in the Journal of Open Source Software, 8(89):5670, September 2023.[^1][^2] The design goal is efficiency: its state-of-the-art polytope algorithms bring most capacity metrics down to a few milliseconds of evaluation time, fast enough for online and interactive use. The package is on PyPI (latest release v2.1.7 as of fetch) and integrates cleanly with standard robotics libraries — `robotics-toolbox`, [[pinocchio]], and `mujoco` — and with human biomechanics tools OpenSim and biorbd, plus ROS; it ships matplotlib-based visualization helpers for fast prototyping.[^1] For robotic manipulators it computes velocity (manipulability), force, and acceleration capacities both as ellipsoids and as polytopes.
