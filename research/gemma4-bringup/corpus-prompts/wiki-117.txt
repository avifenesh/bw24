--- title: Roofline Model type: concept tags: [performance, gpu, profiling, memory-bound, compute-bound, arithmetic-intensity, hardware-squeeze] sources: [] updated: 2026-06-29 --- # Roofline Model The roofline model is a visual performance model that predicts, for a given compute kernel, whether it is limited by the hardware's compute throughput or by its memory bandwidth. It was introduced by Samuel Williams, Andrew Waterman, and David Patterson at Berkeley to make the performance ceilings of throughput-oriented hardware legible at a glance.[^1] The model lives on a log-log plane: the x-axis is **arithmetic intensity** (operations per byte of memory traffic, FLOP/byte), and the y-axis is achieved performance (operations per second). Two hardware-derived "roofs" bound the attainable performance — a flat horizontal **compute roof** equal to the peak FLOP/s of the relevant units (CUDA cores vs Tensor cores have different roofs), and a slanted **memory roof** whose slope equals the peak memory bandwidth (bytes/s).
