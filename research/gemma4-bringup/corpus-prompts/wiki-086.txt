--- title: Gossip Protocols type: concept tags: [distributed-systems, networking, decentralization, failure-detection] sources: [] updated: 2026-06-29 --- Gossip protocols (also called epidemic protocols) are a class of decentralized communication patterns where each node periodically picks a random peer and exchanges state with it, so that information spreads through a cluster the way a rumor or infection spreads through a population.[^1] No node holds a global view and no central coordinator drives the protocol; instead, every round each member gossips to a small random subset of peers, and over successive rounds an update reaches the whole group with high probability. The convergence is probabilistic and *eventually consistent* — given N nodes, a piece of information typically reaches everyone in O(log N) rounds, which is why gossip scales well to large, churning clusters where individual messages are lossy and nodes fail constantly.
