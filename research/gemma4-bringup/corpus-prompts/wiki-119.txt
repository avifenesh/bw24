--- title: Full-Duplex Streaming Interaction type: concept tags: [full-duplex, streaming, spoken-dialogue, turn-taking, omni-modal, latency] sources: [] updated: 2026-06-29 --- # Full-Duplex Streaming Interaction Full-duplex streaming interaction is the system class in which a model listens and speaks at the same time, on a continuous timeline, instead of waiting for a clean turn boundary. The contrast is with the classic half-duplex pipeline — voice activity detection → ASR → text dialogue → TTS — which serializes the interaction, induces several seconds of latency between turns, and loses non-linguistic information (emotion, non-speech sounds) because text is the only intermediate modality.[^1] The defining capability is that the model *always* listens and *always* generates audio (speech or silence), so natural conversational phenomena — pauses, overlapping speech, interruptions, and backchannels ("uh-huh", "mm-hmm") — emerge as simultaneous token streams rather than being handled by explicit turn-taking heuristics.[^1] Moshi (Kyutai, 2024) is the canonical reference implementation: it models two parallel audio streams (one for the model, one for the user) plus an "Inner Monologue" text stream, on top of the streaming Mimi codec, and is described by
