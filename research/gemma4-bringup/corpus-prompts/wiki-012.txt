The two dominant microscaling formats are near-identical on paper (both pack E2M1 with a per-block shared scale) but diverge where it matters. The emergent rule cutting across every section: the format or method that stays on the hardware's native unified-precision matrix datapath wins the end-to-end race, even when a competing scheme has lower offline numerical distortion. ## Key findings - Format design space: MXFP4 uses block size 32, UE8M0 power-of-two scale, 4.25 effective bits, cross-vendor (OCP MX, AMD CDNA4); NVFP4 uses block size 16, E4M3 FP8 scale + per-tensor FP32 scale, 4.5 effective bits, Blackwell-native.
