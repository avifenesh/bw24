--- title: SGLang type: entity status: active tags: [inference, serving, speculative-decoding, eagle3, mtp, radixattention, open-source] sources: [] updated: 2026-06-29 --- # SGLang SGLang (Structured Generation Language) is an open-source, high-performance serving framework for large language and multimodal models, introduced in January 2024 by researchers affiliated with LMSYS, UC Berkeley, Stanford, Texas A&M, and Shanghai Jiao Tong University.[^1][^2] It pairs a Python-embedded front-end language for structured generation (primitives for generation, selection, and parallel control flow) with a high-throughput back-end runtime, and its academic description appeared at NeurIPS 2024.[^2] The runtime's signature optimization is **RadixAttention**, which reuses key-value cache state across generation calls by organizing prefixes in a radix tree, alongside compressed finite-state machines for fast constrained decoding and speculative execution for API-backed models.[^2] Later releases added a zero-overhead CPU batch scheduler, cache-aware load balancing via the SGL Router, and hierarchical KV caching, claiming roughly 1.1x over its prior version and 1.3x over other state-of-the-art baselines in v0.4.[^3] It exposes OpenAI-compatible APIs and runs from a single GPU to large distributed clusters with tensor parallelism.
