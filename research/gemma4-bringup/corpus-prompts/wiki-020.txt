--- title: Raft vs Paxos vs Viewstamped Replication type: concept tags: [distributed-systems, consensus, raft, paxos, viewstamped-replication, quorum] sources: [] updated: 2026-06-29 raw: [raw/learning/agent-knowledge/raft-vs-paxos-vs-viewstamped-replication.md, raw/learning/guides/2026-06-06-raft-vs-paxos-vs-viewstamped-replication.md] --- # Raft vs Paxos vs Viewstamped Replication Multi-Paxos, Viewstamped Replication (VR), and [[raft-consensus|Raft]] are three crash-fault-tolerant (CFT) consensus protocols for building replicated state machines: keep N = 2f+1 servers in lockstep so the group survives f failures while presenting one linearizable copy to clients. The single most important fact is that **they are the same algorithm wearing different clothes** — all reduce to "a stable leader proposes a totally-ordered log of commands, a majority must accept each entry before it commits, and a leader-change protocol re-establishes a leader who possesses every committed entry." Van Renesse, Schiper & Schneider ("Vive la Différence", 2014) proved with refinement mappings that all three refine a common abstract **Multi-Consensus** core but do **not** refine one another — so they are a true family, yet their differences are real and have measurable performance consequences. None tolerates Byzantine faults; for that you need a different family (PBFT, HotStuff, N = 3f+1). All
