--- title: Coupled Quantization type: concept tags: [kv-quant, quantization, vector-quantization, inference, long-context, low-bit, attention] sources: [] updated: 2026-06-29 --- # Coupled Quantization Coupled Quantization (CQ) is a KV cache compression method introduced in the paper "KV Cache is 1 Bit Per Channel: Efficient Large Language Model Inference with Coupled Quantization" by Tianyi Zhang, Jonah Yi, Zhaozhuo Xu, and Anshumali Shrivastava (arXiv:2405.03917, submitted 7 May 2024; NeurIPS 2024).[^1] It attacks an assumption baked into most prior KV quantizers — including the per-channel/per-token recipe on [[kv-cache-quantization]] — that the channels of a key or value embedding should be quantized independently. CQ's central observation is that distinct channels of a K/V activation vector are highly interdependent, so the *joint* entropy of several channels grows more slowly than the sum of their individual (marginal) entropies.
