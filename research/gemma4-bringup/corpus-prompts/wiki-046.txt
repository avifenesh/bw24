--- title: UALink (Ultra Accelerator Link) type: concept tags: [interconnect, scale-up, ualink, ethernet, gpu, accelerator, fabric, open-standard, nvlink-alternative] sources: [] updated: 2026-06-29 --- # UALink (Ultra Accelerator Link) UALink is an open-standard, low-latency, memory-semantic **scale-up** interconnect for connecting AI accelerators (GPUs/XPUs) and switches inside a single server or rack-scale pod. It is the industry's answer to NVIDIA's proprietary NVLink/NVSwitch fabric (see [[nvlink-vs-pcie]]): a multi-vendor consortium standard rather than a single-vendor stack. The UALink Consortium published its first specification, **UALink 200G 1.0**, in April 2025.[^1] The group has ~75 members and was driven by AMD, Broadcom, Cisco, Google, HPE, Intel, Meta, Microsoft, and Synopsys.[^1] The defining goal is to let load/store operations span an entire pod of **up to 1,024 accelerators**, so the aggregate memory of all accelerators appears as a single pool addressable as if locally attached — the same "many GPUs act like one big GPU" promise NVLink delivers, but on an open, Ethernet-derived physical layer.[^1][^2] The spec runs at **200 GT/s per lane** (via the 212.5 Gb/s serial signaling of IEEE P802.3dj), and lanes group into ports: a
