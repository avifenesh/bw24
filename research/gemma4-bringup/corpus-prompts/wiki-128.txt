--- title: On-Policy Distillation type: concept tags: [on-policy-distillation, knowledge-distillation, distillation, reverse-kl, reinforcement-learning, post-training, continual-learning, llm] sources: [] updated: 2026-06-29 --- # On-Policy Distillation On-policy distillation is a post-training recipe that samples trajectories from the *student* model itself and uses a stronger *teacher* model to grade every token of those trajectories. It sits in the corner of the design space that combines two axes Thinking Machines Lab lays out: *sampling* (on-policy = rollouts drawn from the student, off-policy = targets from an external source) and *reward density* (dense vs. sparse).[^1] Supervised fine-tuning is off-policy but dense; reinforcement learning is on-policy but sparse (it teaches roughly O(1) bits per episode — one scalar reward, regardless of token count); on-policy distillation is the remaining on-policy/dense corner, the "best of both worlds," teaching O(N) bits per episode, one per token.[^1] The mechanism is deliberately simple: sample a rollout from the student, run one teacher forward pass to get the teacher's log-probabilities over those exact tokens, and use the **per-token reverse KL** `log π_student − log π_teacher` as a negative reward (advantage).
