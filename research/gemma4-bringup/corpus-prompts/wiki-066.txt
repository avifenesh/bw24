--- title: Conventional Comments type: concept tags: [convention, code-review, github, voice, machine-readable] sources: [] updated: 2026-06-29 --- # Conventional Comments Conventional Comments is a standard for formatting feedback in any review process — code review, peer review, editing, RFCs — by prefixing each comment with a single label.[^1] The proposed format is `<label> [decorations]: <subject>` followed by an optional discussion block.[^1] A label signifies the kind of comment, the subject is the main message, decorations are optional extra classifiers in parentheses, and the discussion carries the "why" and next steps. The site's pitch is that a bare comment like "This is not worded correctly" reads as an unhelpful complaint, while prefixing it with `suggestion:` makes the intent clear and changes the tone, and also prompts the reviewer to give more actionable feedback. Crucially, the labeled form parses cleanly into JSON — `{"label","subject","decorations","discussion"}` — so the comments are machine-readable as well as human-readable.[^1] The strongly-suggested labels are praise, nitpick, suggestion, issue, todo, question, thought, chore, and note, with expressive extras typo, polish, and quibble; teams are free to diverge from the
