--- title: Pearl / vLLM Miner type: entity status: active tags: [pearl, eagle3, speculative-decoding, moe-routing-surgery, quantization-formats, hardware-squeeze, gpu-gemm] sources: [eigen-mem-pearl, eigen-mem-geagpi, eigen-mem-projects] updated: 2026-06-30 --- # Pearl / vLLM Miner Pearl (`~/projects/pearl`) is a vLLM-based crypto/compute miner where the proof-of-work is weight noising baked into the GEMM: every forward, the miner adds a BLAKE3-keyed `noise_A` to the activations and a `noise_B` (`BpEB`) to the weight, then runs a `noisy_gemm` whose result is verifiable host-side. Upstream is `pearl-research-labs/pearl`; [[avi]] works from fork `avifenesh/pearl` with the default integration branch `master`. The serving target is Pearl-8B (`pearl-ai/Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct-pearl`) on H100 sm_90a (Hopper, 228KB SMEM), and the whole effort is a [[hardware-squeeze]] problem: the noising tax is ~6x throughput (mining ON 4.09 vs OFF 24.35 req/s), with `noise_B` alone accounting for ~93% of decode mining cost at ~5x the plain matmul.
