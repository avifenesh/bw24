--- title: LMCache KV Cache Layer type: concept tags: [inference, serving, kv-cache, vllm, sglang, prefix-caching, offloading, pd-disaggregation, open-source] sources: [] updated: 2026-06-29 --- # LMCache KV Cache Layer LMCache is an open-source, vendor-neutral KV cache management layer for LLM inference, originally developed at the University of Chicago and now backed by Tensormesh Inc.[^1][^2] Its core idea is to treat the KV cache not as transient per-request GPU state but as reusable "AI-native knowledge" that can be stored persistently, reused across queries and across serving-engine instances, monitored, and transformed.[^1] LMCache extracts the KV caches produced by mainstream engines (vLLM and SGLang) out of GPU memory and offloads them into a tiered storage hierarchy spanning CPU RAM, local SSD, and remote backends, so that historical context does not have to be re-prefilled.[^1][^2] It runs as an engine-independent daemon process, meaning the cache is not lost if the inference engine crashes (no fate-sharing), and it joined the PyTorch Foundation ecosystem in late 2025.[^1] The headline benefit is reduced time-to-first-token and higher throughput on long-context, multi-turn agentic, and knowledge-augmented (RAG) workloads — the original
