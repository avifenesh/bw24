The problem is posed probabilistically: a **state** $x$ evolves through a **process model** (with process noise $Q$), measurements $z$ relate to the state through a **measurement model** (with measurement noise $R$), and the goal is the posterior $p(x \mid z)$ — or, for the tractable filters, its mean and covariance. This underpins localization, mapping, dynamic-object tracking, and sensor fusion (IMU + GPS + LiDAR + camera). Even a perfect controller fails in the real world without it. The canonical reference is Thrun, Burgard & Fox, *Probabilistic Robotics*; Roger Labbe's *Kalman and Bayesian Filters in Python* is the practical companion.
