--- title: Novelty-Utility Tradeoff type: concept tags: [creativity, novelty, ideation, llm-evaluation, combinatorial-creativity] sources: [] updated: 2026-06-29 --- # Novelty-Utility Tradeoff The novelty-utility tradeoff (also called novelty-value) is the structural tension in any creative generation system between producing ideas that are *new* (far from what already exists) and ideas that are *useful* (feasible, grounded, valuable). A creative act is conventionally defined as one that is both novel and valuable, so the two criteria must be satisfied jointly rather than traded off one against the other — yet optimizing hard for either tends to erode the other. Push for novelty and outputs drift into noise or infeasibility; push for utility and outputs collapse back toward safe, familiar territory.[^1] The 2025-09 framework paper "Combinatorial Creativity: A New Frontier in Generalization Abilities" formalizes combinatorial creativity as an open-ended generalization ability that must be evaluated jointly on novelty and utility rather than against fixed reference targets, and reports that the tradeoff is **persistent even as model scale grows** — larger models do not simply dissolve it.[^1] The paper identifies this tradeoff as the deeper cause
