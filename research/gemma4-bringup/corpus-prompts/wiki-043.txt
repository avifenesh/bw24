--- title: RadixArk type: entity status: active tags: [inference, serving, startup, sglang, reinforcement-learning, gpu-infrastructure] sources: [] updated: 2026-06-29 --- # RadixArk RadixArk is the venture-backed commercial company behind [[sglang|SGLang]], the high-performance open-source LLM inference engine. It originated as the SGLang project in 2023 inside the UC Berkeley lab of Databricks co-founder Ion Stoica — the same lab that incubated [[vllm|vLLM]] — and was formally launched as a startup in August 2025.[^1] Its name is a play on SGLang's signature optimization, RadixAttention (radix-tree key-value-cache prefix reuse). The co-founder and CEO is Ying Sheng, a key SGLang contributor and former research scientist at Databricks who left xAI to start the company.[^1] In January 2026 RadixArk was reported to have raised a round led by Accel at a valuation of about $400M, after earlier angel capital from investors including Intel CEO Lip-Bu Tan.[^1] RadixArk continues to develop SGLang as a free, open-source model engine while charging fees for hosting/inference services on top of it; SGLang is used by companies such as xAI and Cursor to accelerate AI workloads.[^1] Beyond serving, the company is
