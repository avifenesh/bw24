Expand on next relevant ingest. "Long context" is the regime where prompt/history length (tens of thousands to millions of tokens) rather than model size becomes the binding constraint — attention cost and KV-cache memory both grow with sequence length, so this is where sparse/linear attention, cache compression, and position-encoding scaling all earn their keep. Hub pointers: - **Attention cost** — [[subquadratic-attention]] is the survey hub; [[native-sparse-attention]], [[sliding-window-attention]], [[ring-attention]], [[linear-attention]] are the main escape routes. - **KV-cache memory** — [[kv-cache-management]], [[kv-cache-quantization]], [[multi-head-latent-attention]] (architectural compression), [[kv-cache-offloading]]. - **Position encoding** — [[rope]] scaling ([[yarn-rope-scaling]]) and [[length-generalization]] govern whether a model *works* at lengths beyond training. - **Evaluation** — [[ruler-long-context-benchmark]] stress-tests claimed windows; advertised context ≠ usable context. - **Owner ground truth** — [[bw24]]'s long-context arm: 32k prompts in 19.4GB, a 278k-token prompt run exact on 24GB, and the observed spec-decode decay with context (1.57x @ 6k
