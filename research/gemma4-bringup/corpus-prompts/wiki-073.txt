--- title: HASS (Harmonized Speculative Sampling) type: entity tags: [speculative-decoding, eagle, draft-model, context-alignment, iclr-2025, paper] sources: [] updated: 2026-06-29 --- # HASS (Harmonized Speculative Sampling) HASS — **HArmonized Speculative Sampling** — is a draft-model training method published at ICLR 2025 by a team at Xiaohongshu Inc. (Lefan Zhang, Xiaodan Wang, Yanhua Huang, Ruiwen Xu), arXiv 2408.15766.[^1][^2] It is a direct successor to EAGLE-2: like EAGLE, it drafts from the target model's contextual information (hidden states and KV cache) rather than from a separate small model, but it fixes two discrepancies that the authors identify in EAGLE-style drafters. The first is a **train/decode context mismatch**: during training the drafter sees the target model's real hidden states as input, but at decode time it must consume its own previously-drafted (and possibly wrong) hidden states, so the distribution it is conditioned on at runtime differs from the one it was trained on.
