--- title: Multi-Turn Prefix Caching type: concept tags: [inference, serving, kv-cache, prefix-caching, multi-turn, vllm, sglang, radixattention] sources: [] updated: 2026-06-29 --- # Multi-Turn Prefix Caching Multi-turn prefix caching is the technique of reusing the KV cache computed for the shared leading tokens of a conversation across successive turns, instead of recomputing attention over that context every time. In a chat or agentic workload each new request resends the entire history — system prompt, prior user turns, prior model replies — followed by a short new user message. Because attention is causal, the KV state for those leading tokens is identical to what was computed on the previous turn, so a server that keeps it around can skip the expensive prefill over thousands of context tokens and only prefill the small new suffix. This turns the dominant cost of multi-turn serving (re-prefilling a growing prefix) into a cache lookup, cutting time-to-first-token and freeing compute for other requests.
