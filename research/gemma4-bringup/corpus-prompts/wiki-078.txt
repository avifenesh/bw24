Expand on next relevant ingest. Chain-of-thought (CoT) is the practice of having a language model produce intermediate reasoning tokens before its final answer — originally a prompting trick ("let's think step by step", Wei et al. 2022), now the substrate of reasoning models: RL post-training ([[rlvr]], [[reinforcement-learning-verifiable-rewards]]) *elicits and lengthens* CoT because verifiable rewards credit trajectories that reason their way to checkable answers. Where it connects in this wiki: - **Test-time compute** — CoT length is the main axis [[test-time-scaling]] scales along; [[reasoning-effort]] is the serving-side knob that budgets it. - **Training** — [[reinforcement-learning-llm]] and [[grpo]]-family methods optimize over sampled CoT rollouts; distillation of reasoning traces appears in [[knowledge-distillation]]. - **Safety/monitoring** — [[cot-monitorability]] covers whether the visible chain faithfully reflects the computation, and the pressure RL puts on that faithfulness. ## Related Concepts: [[test-time-scaling]], [[reasoning-effort]], [[rlvr]], [[reinforcement-learning-verifiable-rewards]], [[reinforcement-learning-llm]], [[grpo]], [[cot-monitorability]], [[knowledge-distillation]]
