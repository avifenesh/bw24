--- title: MixFP4 type: concept tags: [quantization, fp4, int4, nvfp4, e2m1, e1m2, ptq, microscaling, blackwell, mixed-format] sources: [] updated: 2026-06-30 --- # MixFP4 **MixFP4** ("Enhancing NVFP4 with Adaptive FP4/INT4 Block Representations", a 2026 paper, May 29 2026) is a post-training-quantization (PTQ) format that extends [[nvfp4-production|NVFP4]] by letting each 1×16 micro-block choose, per-block, between two 4-bit element formats: the standard non-uniform **E2M1** grid and a uniform **E1M2** (INT-like) grid.[^1] The premise is that **no single FP4 micro-format is universally optimal across all blocks and modules**, because block-level tensor statistics are heterogeneous. Outlier-heavy blocks favor the exponent-rich, dynamic-range-friendly spacing of E2M1, while "flat" blocks dominated by small-magnitude values favor the even spacing of a uniform INT4-style grid — and applying a Hadamard transform reshapes which of the two is preferable for a given block. MixFP4 turns that observation into an adaptive choice made at the granularity of NVFP4's existing 1×16 block.[^1] The engineering trick that makes this cheap is metadata-free format selection. The per-block choice between E2M1 and E1M2 is encoded in the **redundant sign bit of the FP8 E4M3 block scale**
