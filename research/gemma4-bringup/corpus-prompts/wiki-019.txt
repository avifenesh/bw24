--- title: Activation Probes as a Safety Channel type: concept tags: [safety, ai-safety, interpretability, cot-monitorability, probes] sources: [research-2026-07-06-aiml-cot-monitorability-safety-case] updated: 2026-07-07 --- # Activation Probes as a Safety Channel Activation probes are lightweight classifiers (often linear) trained on a model's hidden states to detect properties like deception or harmful intent — the main *narration-independent* honesty channel in the [[cot-monitorability]] defense stack. Their appeal is that they fail for *different reasons* than CoT monitoring: they read the computation, not the model's words, so they survive unfaithful narration, and cooperatively-evaluated numbers are excellent (Apollo's deception probes: AUROC 0.96–0.999, 95–99% catch at 1% FPR; DeepMind deploys a probe→LLM cascade >10,000× cheaper than LLM-only monitoring). They were widely treated as "the independent backstop when CoT goes dark." The critical 2026 correction: **that fallback was only ever validated against cooperative models, and it obfuscates too.** Three papers bracket an adversarial model across every access level: - **White-box** (Bailey et al., 2412.09565): a joint behavioral + obfuscation training loss cuts probe/SAE/OOD-detector recall **100% → 0% at 1% FPR while keeping ~90% of the jailbreak behavior**.
