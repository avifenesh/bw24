--- title: Parallel Drafting type: concept tags: [inference, speculative-decoding, eagle3, parallel-drafting, diffusion, mtp] sources: [] updated: 2026-06-29 --- # Parallel Drafting **Parallel drafting** is the class of speculative-decoding techniques that produce all of a cycle's draft tokens in a *single* draft forward pass, instead of generating them one at a time. The contrast is with autoregressive drafters like [[eagle3]] and native [[mtp]] heads, where each additional speculated token requires another sequential forward pass through the draft model — so drafting latency grows roughly linearly with speculation depth, and methods keep their drafters very shallow to hold latency down, which in turn caps draft quality.[^1] Autoregressive token-by-token generation also has low arithmetic intensity, a poor fit for GPUs and TPUs that prefer large parallel matrix work.[^1] Parallel drafting attacks this directly: by emitting a whole block of K draft tokens in one pass, it decouples the draft-token count from the forward-pass count, which is the same sequential coupling that a control approach like [[learning-to-draft]]'s depth policy has to ration around. Because deeper drafting becomes cheap, the optimal speculation depth shifts upward.[^2]
