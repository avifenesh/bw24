--- title: Research 2026-07-05 — Late-Interaction Retrieval in 2026 (when the multi-vector tax is worth paying) type: source tags: [retrieval, late-interaction, colbert, muvera, reranking, research] updated: 2026-07-06 raw: raw/papers/research-2026-07-05-aiml-late-interaction-retrieval.md --- # Research 2026-07-05 — Late-Interaction Retrieval in 2026 A 41-source synthesis of the ColBERT-family literature, organized into four mental models. Central thesis: late interaction's *quality* argument is settled (out-of-domain, reasoning, multimodal); the entire live frontier is efficiency, storage, and infrastructure fit — and the production answer, converged on independently by six vector databases, is that **late interaction should be a reranker, not a primary index**. Extends the wiki's existing late-interaction hub ([[late-interaction-scaling]]) with the production verdict, the three-way infra fork, and a genuinely new axis: the scoring operator itself as a learnable design choice. ## Key takeaways - **Mental model 1 — MaxSim is a cheap fixed nonlinearity bolted onto a two-tower.** `s(q,d) = Σᵢ maxⱼ (qᵢ·dⱼ)` (Google calls it Chamfer similarity). The `max` is both the power and the blow-up: any query token can match any doc token, so the doc side can't be pre-reduced — hence 100+ vectors/doc.
