--- title: Deterministic Simulation Testing (DST) type: concept tags: [testing, distributed-systems, determinism, fault-injection, databases, reliability] sources: [] updated: 2026-06-29 --- # Deterministic Simulation Testing (DST) Deterministic Simulation Testing is a technique for finding bugs in distributed and concurrent systems by running the **real code** — not a model of it — inside a controlled, fully deterministic environment where every source of non-determinism is owned by the test harness. The whole distributed system (multiple nodes, their disk, network, OS interactions) runs in a **single process on a single thread**, with simulated concurrency. All randomness flows from one PRNG seed the simulator controls, and the clock is also under the simulator's control, so a run is reproducible: observe a bad state, re-enter the same seed, and the entire execution that led to the fault replays exactly.[^1][^2] On top of this the simulator injects faults — dropped/delayed/duplicated packets, disk errors and corruption, process crashes, whole datacenter failures — at a *configurable* regularity, so failures that occur every few years in production can be made to happen every few minutes in simulation.[^1][^2] The pitch
