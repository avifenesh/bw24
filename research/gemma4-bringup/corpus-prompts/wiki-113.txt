--- title: FlashAttention Quant Kernels (llama.cpp CUDA) type: concept tags: [flashattention, llama-cpp, cuda, kv-quant, gotcha, prefill] sources: [llamacpp-fa-quant-kernels, qwen36-27b-server-config] updated: 2026-06-29 --- # FlashAttention Quant Kernels (llama.cpp CUDA) A hidden gate in llama.cpp's CUDA FlashAttention: by default it ships kernels for only 4 SYMMETRIC K/V dtype pairs — F16/F16, Q4_0/Q4_0, Q8_0/Q8_0, and BF16/BF16. Any asymmetric K/V combination silently falls back to a non-FA path that collapses prefill by roughly 18x (q8/q8 ~1000 tok/s vs q8/q4_1 ~54 tok/s @ 70k context), with no warning emitted. ## Detection and fix - Detection: prefill below ~300 tok/s at depth indicates the FA fallback is engaged. - Fix: rebuild with `-DGGML_CUDA_FA_ALL_QUANTS=ON`, which compiles all 49 K/V combos. ## Why it matters here This is the hidden gate behind the [[qwen36-27b-server]]'s K=q8_0 / V=q5_1 asymmetric KV choice (see [[quantization-formats]]). Without the all-quants build, that config would silently run on the slow fallback path. The server's llama.cpp build was rebuilt with FA_ALL_QUANTS on 2026-06-02 specifically to unlock the asymmetric KV pair.
