--- title: Prefill/Decode Disaggregation type: concept tags: [inference, serving, pd-disaggregation, kv-cache, goodput, distserve, dynamo] sources: [] updated: 2026-06-29 --- # Prefill/Decode Disaggregation LLM inference has two phases with opposite hardware appetites. **Prefill** processes the whole prompt in parallel to build the KV cache and emit the first token — large matmuls, compute-bound, light on memory. **Decode** then generates output tokens one at a time, reloading the model weights and an ever-growing KV cache each step — memory-bandwidth-bound, light on compute. The standard "colocated" design (continuous batching as popularized by Orca and early vLLM) runs both phases on the same GPUs, but they interfere: a single large prefill stalls in-flight decodes (chunked prefill softens this but can still inflate per-token latency 2–30× under bursty load), and because prefill drives TTFT while decode drives ITL/TPOT, one resource pool must be provisioned for the worst case of both SLOs at once. **PD disaggregation** splits the two phases onto separate GPU pools connected by a KV-cache transfer path.
