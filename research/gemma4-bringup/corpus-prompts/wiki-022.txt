--- title: Differentiable Simulation type: concept tags: [robotics, simulation, autodiff, gradients, control, physics] sources: [] updated: 2026-06-29 --- # Differentiable Simulation Differentiable simulation brings the automatic-differentiation paradigm to physics. Instead of treating a simulator as a black box that maps state and controls to a next state, the entire forward dynamics are implemented inside an autodiff framework (PyTorch, JAX, TensorFlow, or a JIT like Taichi/Warp) so that gradients of the output trajectory with respect to initial position, velocity, control inputs, and physical parameters (mass, friction, stiffness) can be computed by backpropagation through time.[^1][^2] Those analytic gradients are what make the simulator useful as a *layer*: a differentiable simulator can sit inside a larger learning pipeline, letting first-order optimizers solve trajectory optimization, system identification, inverse design, and control problems far more sample-efficiently than zeroth-order/black-box methods such as model-free RL or finite differences.
