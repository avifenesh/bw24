--- title: AITER type: entity status: active tags: [amd, rocm, kernels, gpu, inference, training] sources: [] updated: 2026-06-29 --- # AITER AITER (AI Tensor Engine for ROCm) is AMD's centralized, open-source library of high-performance AI operators — fused GPU kernels for both inference and training on AMD Instinct GPUs via the [[rocm]] stack.[^1][^2] It is the AMD counterpart to NVIDIA's cuDNN/[[transformer-engine]] kernel libraries: a single repository (`github.com/ROCm/aiter`) that collects optimized implementations of the operators that dominate transformer workloads — GEMM, fused Mixture-of-Experts, multi-head attention (Flash-Attention-style prefill, paged-attention decode), MLA decode, RoPE, LayerNorm/RMSNorm, and collective communication kernels.[^2] Operators are reachable through two interfaces: a C++ API and a Python (Torch) API, with kernels built underneath on a mix of Triton, AMD's Composable Kernel (CK), hand-written assembly (ASM), and HIP.[^2] AMD published the initial AITER blog and repository in March 2025, positioning it as a drop-in source of tuned kernels that frameworks can integrate without rewriting their own ROCm paths.[^2] Reported per-operator gains on a single MI300X (AMD's own benchmarks, March 2025) include up to 2x for block-scale GEMM, up to 3x
