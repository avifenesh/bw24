--- title: Mooncake type: entity status: active tags: [kv-cache, disaggregation, transfer-engine, rdma, llm-serving, moonshot, kimi] sources: [research-2026-06-25-aiml-rust-inference-engine-anatomy, research-2026-06-18-aiml-kv-transfer-disaggregated-serving] updated: 2026-06-29 --- # Mooncake Mooncake is the open-source serving platform behind **Kimi**, the LLM service from Moonshot AI, and has become the de-facto industry substrate for moving and pooling KV cache across an inference cluster (`github.com/kvcache-ai/Mooncake`, Apache-2.0).[^1] Its defining idea — earning it the **FAST '25 Best Paper Award** — is a *KVCache-centric disaggregated architecture*: prefill and decode run as separate clusters, and the otherwise-underutilized CPU, DRAM, SSD, and NIC resources of the GPU fleet are pooled into a single disaggregated KVCache layer scheduled to maximize effective throughput under latency SLOs (with a prediction-based early-rejection policy for overload).[^2] The reported payoff is large in long-context regimes — up to **525% throughput** in simulation versus the baseline, and **75% more requests** handled by Kimi under real production workloads.[^2] The system is framed as "trading more storage for less computation": store and reuse KV rather than recompute prefixes.[^2] Two open-sourced components carry that thesis and are what downstream engines actually bind.
