--- title: Nemotron type: entity tags: [nvidia, open-models, moe, mamba-transformer, nvfp4, reasoning, agentic, quantization] sources: [] updated: 2026-06-29 --- # Nemotron Nemotron is NVIDIA's family of open-weight large language models, positioned for agentic reasoning, math, coding, and conversational use. The current generation, **Nemotron 3** (announced December 2025), ships in three sizes — **Nano**, **Super**, and **Ultra** — sharing a hybrid Mamba-Transformer mixture-of-experts (MoE) architecture that trades the standard Transformer's quadratic attention for SSM ([[jamba-hybrid-ssm]]) layers to win throughput at long context.[^1] Nemotron 3 Nano is a 31.6B-total / 3.2B-active MoE (3.6B with embeddings) that NVIDIA reports as more accurate than GPT-OSS-20B and Qwen3-30B-A3B-Thinking-2507 across mixed benchmarks, while delivering 3.3x the inference throughput of Qwen3-30B-A3B and 2.2x that of GPT-OSS-20B on a single H200 at the 8K-in/16K-out setting, with a native 1M-token context window.[^1] Super and Ultra add **LatentMoE** (a hardware-aware expert design), Multi-Token Prediction (MTP) layers for faster long-form generation, and are **trained natively in NVFP4**; all three are post-trained with multi-environment reinforcement learning and inference-time reasoning-budget control.[^1] NVIDIA releases weights, the training recipe, and the redistributable pretraining/SFT/RL data ([[nvfp4-pretraining-recipe]]
