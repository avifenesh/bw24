Reinforcement learning is the framework where an agent learns a policy by acting in an environment and optimizing cumulative reward, rather than imitating labeled examples. In this wiki it shows up in three distinct guises, each with its own hub page: 1. **LLM post-training** — the dominant use here. [[reinforcement-learning-llm]] is the hub; [[ppo]]-lineage policy-gradient methods, [[grpo]] and its variant zoo ([[gspo]]), preference methods ([[dpo]]), and verifiable-reward setups ([[rlvr]], [[reinforcement-learning-verifiable-rewards]]) all descend from it. The standing hazards are [[reward-hacking]] and reward-model overoptimization ([[reward-model]]). 2. **Serving-time control** — lighter-weight bandit formulations for runtime decisions, e.g. [[contextual-bandit]] speculation-length controllers. 3. **Robotics** — learned locomotion and manipulation policies; see [[legged-robot-locomotion]].
