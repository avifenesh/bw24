--- title: Lyanna type: concept tags: [inference, speculative-decoding, hidden-state, draft-model, eagle3, compute-efficiency] sources: [] updated: 2026-06-29 --- # Lyanna **Lyanna** ("Make Every Draft Count: Hidden State based Speculative Decoding," arXiv 2602.21224, Yuetao Chen et al., CUHK / Waterloo / USTC, Feb 2026) is a [[speculative-decoding]] system whose thesis is that tree-based drafting wastes most of the compute it spends. In tree drafting the draft model generates many candidate tokens across multiple branches, the target verifies the whole tree in parallel, and then **only the tokens on the single accepted path are kept — every token on a rejected branch is thrown away, along with the hidden states that produced it**.[^1] The deeper drafts grow, the more this waste compounds: branching factor and depth both multiply the discarded count. Standard speculative decoding raises arithmetic intensity (good for memory-bound decode) at the cost of this compute inefficiency, and the prior frontier mostly responds by *suppressing* speculation under load (shrink the draft length, switch the draft off — see [[adaptive-spec-batching]]).
