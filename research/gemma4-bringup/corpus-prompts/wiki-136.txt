--- title: FP4 All the Way type: concept tags: [quantization, nvfp4, fp4, fully-quantized-training, fqt, gradients, stochastic-rounding, gaudi] sources: [] updated: 2026-06-29 --- # FP4 All the Way "FP4 All the Way: Fully Quantized Training of LLMs" is a May 2025 paper by Brian Chmiel, Maxim Fishman, Ron Banner, and Daniel Soudry (Intel / NVIDIA-Israel / Technion lineage), submitted 25 May 2025 and revised 10 Aug 2025 (v2).[^1] Its claim to firsts is fully quantized training (FQT) of an LLM with weights, activations, *and* gradients carried predominantly in 4-bit floating point, demonstrated on datasets up to 200 billion tokens — where most "FP4 training" work (including NVIDIA's own [[nvfp4-pretraining-recipe]]) keeps attention and a fraction of linear layers in higher precision. The headline result: a 7-billion-parameter model trained on 256 Intel Gaudi2 accelerators reaches downstream-task performance comparable to a standard BF16 baseline, presented as evidence that FP4 training is practical and efficient at scale.[^1] A reference implementation is published on GitHub.[^1] The paper's design sweep over block sizes, scaling formats, and rounding methods lands on conclusions that independently corroborate the NVIDIA recipe.
