--- title: Pearl Research Labs type: entity status: active tags: [pearl, organization, proof-of-useful-work, crypto, gpu-gemm, omri-weinstein] sources: [eigen-mem-pearl] updated: 2026-06-29 --- # Pearl Research Labs Pearl Research Labs is the organization behind the **Pearl network** (ticker PRL), a Layer-1 blockchain that secures itself with **Proof-of-Useful-Work (PoUW)**: instead of SHA-256 hashing, miners run large-scale integer matrix multiplication — the operation Pearl calls **NoisyGEMM** — hash the result into a commitment, and wrap it in a cheap-to-verify proof.[^1][^2] The lab is led by co-founder and CEO **Omri Weinstein**, a complexity theorist (Princeton PhD, ex-Columbia TCS group, currently at the Hebrew University, listing prior time at Nvidia and Vast Data).[^2] Weinstein is also a co-author of the cryptography paper the protocol rests on — *"Proofs of Useful Work from Arbitrary Matrix Multiplication"* (Komargodski, Schen, and Weinstein, arXiv:2504.09971, April 2025) — and Pearl's implementation of that scheme is termed **cuPOW**, claiming only ~1+o(1) overhead versus a naive matmul (the "2-for-1" pitch of one GPU pass producing both the chain commitment and useful compute).[^2][^3] The lab authors and publishes the upstream monorepo `pearl-research-labs/pearl` (ISC-licensed), which
