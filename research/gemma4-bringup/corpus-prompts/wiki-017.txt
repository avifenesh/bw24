--- title: Elastic Expert Parallelism type: concept tags: [inference, serving, moe, expert-parallelism, load-balancing, autoscaling, fault-tolerance] sources: [] updated: 2026-06-29 --- # Elastic Expert Parallelism In a [[wide-ep-moe-serving|wide expert-parallel]] MoE deployment, the set of experts is sharded across a fixed group of GPUs (the EP group), and a runtime placement plan — typically [[eplb|EPLB]] — decides which logical experts (and their redundant replicas) live on which rank so that per-rank token load stays balanced. That machinery assumes a *static membership*: the number of ranks is fixed for the life of the server. **Elastic expert parallelism** is the extension that lets the EP group's membership change while the model keeps serving — ranks can be added on scale-up, removed on scale-down, or drop out on failure, and the expert placement is re-derived and re-shuffled in place rather than requiring a full restart. It is the natural marriage of EPLB's online rebalancing primitive (which already recomputes a logical-to-physical expert mapping and shuffles weights without restarting the model)[^1] with the autoscaling and fault-tolerance needs of a production cluster.
