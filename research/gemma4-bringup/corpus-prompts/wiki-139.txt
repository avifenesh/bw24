--- title: Expert Parallelism Serving type: concept tags: [moe, inference, serving, expert-parallelism, all-to-all, disaggregation] sources: [] updated: 2026-06-30 --- # Expert Parallelism Serving Expert parallelism (EP) is the deployment strategy that makes large sparse Mixture-of-Experts models servable: the N expert FFNs of each layer are sharded across many GPUs/nodes, and at every layer tokens are dispatched to whichever device holds their chosen experts and the outputs are gathered back. It exists because a frontier MoE — DeepSeek-V3/R1 at 671B total parameters, Kimi K2 at ~1T — cannot fit, and cannot be served with enough memory bandwidth, on a single node.[^1] During decode each token activates only a small expert subset per layer, so on one node the experts would be starved of batch and the GPU memory-bandwidth-bound; spreading experts across many devices lets each expert accumulate enough tokens from a large global batch to run efficiently. This is the serving counterpart to the training-time "expert parallelism" sharding noted in [[moe-routing-algorithms]], but the binding constraint is different: at inference the dominant cost is the **all-to-all** dispatch/combine communication between devices, not FLOPs,
