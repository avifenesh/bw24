--- title: Swamping type: concept tags: [numerical-analysis, floating-point, low-precision, accumulation, training] sources: [] updated: 2026-06-29 --- **Swamping** is a floating-point rounding failure that happens when a small value is added to a much larger one and the small value is lost entirely. The term comes from Nicholas Higham's numerical-analysis work on the accuracy and stability of summation: when two numbers differ in magnitude by more than the available precision, the smaller operand falls off the low end of the larger operand's mantissa, so round-to-nearest snaps the computed sum straight back to the larger value unchanged. The small term is said to be *swamped* by the large one. The classic illustration is repeated summation — adding a long run of tiny increments to a growing accumulator. Once the running total is large enough that each individual increment rounds to zero against it, the sum stops moving even though, in exact arithmetic, the increments should keep contributing.
