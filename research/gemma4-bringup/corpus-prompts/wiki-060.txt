The thesis: Blackwell (SM100/B200) is not "Hopper with bigger tensor cores" — NVIDIA changed the software contract (MMA results moved to a software-managed Tensor Memory, MMA became single-thread/CTA-scoped, a 2-SM collaborative MMA appeared), and the binding constraint moved off the tensor cores. Every hard problem is now a feeding problem. ## Key findings - Three coupled hardware changes: accumulators left the register file for TMEM (per-SM 256KB, 128 lanes × 512 columns, ~16 TB/s read); MMA issued by one thread on behalf of the whole CTA; cta_group::2 runs one MMA across two SMs. Touching TMEM hard-caps occupancy to 1 block/SM. tcgen05.mma issue latency 11.0 cyc vs wgmma 32.0 cyc (~3× lower), near-flat as tile size grows 16×. - Consumer/datacenter fork: tcgen05/TMEM/clusters exist only on SM100 (datacenter B200); consumer Blackwell (SM120, RTX 50) gets the same 5th-gen tensor-core
