--- title: Selection Vectors and Chunk Compaction type: concept tags: [databases, olap, query-execution, hardware-squeeze, adaptivity] sources: [research-2026-06-15-systems-vectorized-compaction-selection-vectors, research-2026-06-17-systems-columnar-encoding-decode] updated: 2026-06-29 --- # Selection Vectors and Chunk Compaction Vectorized engines process fixed-size batches (DuckDB's 2048-row DataChunk, ClickHouse blocks, Velox RowVector, Photon column batches) in branch-free SIMD loops, and the whole performance model rests on one unstated assumption — **the vectors stay full**. A selective filter or a hash-join probe leaves a 2048-slot container holding a handful of live rows, so every downstream operator pays its full fixed per-`Next()` cost over near-empty data. Made concrete (Qiao & Zhang, SIGMOD'25, DuckDB v1.1.3): **39% of post-join chunks contain exactly ONE live tuple** (chunk-reducing-factor = 2048); a 4-table probe pipeline compounds to 1.25 tuples/chunk, each downstream operator paying its fixed cost ~1600× over almost no data. Engines have exactly two structural levers — carry a **selection vector** (an index of live rows: no data movement, but the gaps defeat SIMD) or **compact/flatten** (restore density, pay a copy/gather cost).
