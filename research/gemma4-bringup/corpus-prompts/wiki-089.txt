--- title: GB200 NVL72 type: entity tags: [hardware, nvidia, blackwell, nvlink, rack-scale, moe, wide-ep, inference] sources: [research-2026-06-05-wide-ep-moe-serving, research-2026-05-27-blackwell-stack-moat] updated: 2026-06-29 --- # GB200 NVL72 The NVIDIA GB200 NVL72 is a liquid-cooled, rack-scale Blackwell system that wires **72 Blackwell GPUs and 36 Grace CPUs** into a single coherent compute domain.[^1] Its defining feature is the **72-GPU NVLink domain** — the largest NVLink fabric NVIDIA has shipped — driven by the fifth-generation NVLink Switch System, which gives every GPU 1.8 TB/s of GPU-to-GPU bandwidth and an aggregate **130 TB/s** of low-latency interconnect across the rack (72 × 1.8 TB/s).[^1][^2] Physically the rack is 18 compute trays, each holding two GB200 Grace Blackwell Superchips; a Superchip pairs two Blackwell GPUs with one Grace CPU over a 900 GB/s NVLink-C2C link, so the CPU:GPU ratio is 1:2.[^2] The full rack delivers on the order of 1.44 exaFLOPS of FP4 (with sparsity) and ~13.4 TB of unified GPU memory, and its second-generation Transformer Engine adds native FP4 (NVFP4) microscaling support — see [[nvfp4-production]] and [[blackwell-architecture]] for the precision story.[^1][^2] NVIDIA markets it as a system
