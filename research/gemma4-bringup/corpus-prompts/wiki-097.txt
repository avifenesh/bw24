A rigid body in 3-D has **6 DoF** (3 translation + 3 rotation); joints constrain those. The **Grübler/Kutzbach formula** counts a mechanism's mobility: in 3-D, $M = 6(N-1-j) + \sum f_i$ (use 3 instead of 6 in the planar case), where $N$ counts rigid bodies including the fixed base and $j$ the joints. A robot with $n$ joints is under-actuated, exactly-actuated, or **redundant** ($n >$ task DoF, giving infinite IK solutions usable for obstacle avoidance) relative to the 6-DoF task space $SE(3)$.
