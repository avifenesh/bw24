--- title: TPOT SLO type: concept tags: [inference, serving, slo, latency, goodput, scheduling, metrics] sources: [] updated: 2026-06-29 --- # TPOT SLO A **TPOT SLO** is a service-level objective placed on **Time Per Output Token** — the average time gap between consecutive generated tokens during the steady decode phase, excluding the first token. TPOT is typically computed as `(E2EL − TTFT) / (output_tokens − 1)`, where E2EL is end-to-end latency and TTFT is time-to-first-token; for a single request the mean of all inter-token latencies (ITL) equals TPOT, which is why the two terms are often used interchangeably.[^1] An SLO turns this metric into a contract: a target threshold a request's decoding speed must hold to count as well-served, e.g. "95% of chatbot tokens stream with TPOT below 50 ms."
