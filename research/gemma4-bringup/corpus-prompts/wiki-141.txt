--- title: Hyper-Connections type: concept tags: [residual-connections, architecture, pretraining, transformer, training-stability, bytedance, deepseek] sources: [] updated: 2026-06-29 --- # Hyper-Connections Hyper-connections (HC) are a drop-in replacement for the residual connection, introduced by ByteDance's Seed-Foundation-Model team (Defa Zhu et al., arXiv 2409.19606, published Sept 2024, accepted to ICLR 2025).[^1] A standard residual stream is a single tensor that every layer reads from and writes back to via a fixed-weight identity add; the paper frames its central weakness as a **seesaw effect** — tuning the residual weight to fight gradient vanishing pushes the network toward representation collapse, and vice versa, so no single static choice is good.[^1] Hyper-connections instead split the residual stream into `n` parallel copies (the **expansion rate**) and let the network learn how those copies mix.
