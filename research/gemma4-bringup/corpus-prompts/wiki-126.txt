--- title: Ablation Study type: concept tags: [research-method, evaluation, methodology, interpretability] sources: [] updated: 2026-06-29 --- An **ablation study** is an experimental method that measures the contribution of a single component of an AI system by removing (or disabling) that component and then observing how the system's performance changes.[^1] The term is borrowed from biology — the surgical removal of a part of an organism — and entered AI by analogy with ablative brain surgery; it is credited to Allen Newell, who used it in a 1974 tutorial on speech recognition.[^1] The motivation Newell gave still holds: individual components are deliberately engineered, but their *actual* contribution to end-to-end system performance is not obvious, and stripping a component out is the cleanest way to isolate that contribution.[^1] In modern deep-learning practice an ablation typically toggles off one design choice at a time — a loss term, an architectural block, a data-augmentation step, a quantization scheme — while holding everything else fixed, so that any measured delta can be attributed to that one variable.
