--- title: Perfect-Blend Dataset type: entity tags: [dataset, instruction-tuning, speculative-decoding, eagle3, training-data] sources: [eagle3-nvfp4-specforge] updated: 2026-06-29 --- # Perfect-Blend Dataset Perfect-Blend (most commonly the open reproduction **Open-PerfectBlend**) is a ~1.4M-sample general-purpose instruction-tuning corpus that the [[specforge]] team adopted as the training data for its production [[specbundle|SpecBundle]] [[eagle3|EAGLE3]] draft heads. The dataset originates from the LLM-mix described in the paper *The Perfect Blend: Redefining RLHF with Mixture of Judges* (arXiv:2409.20370), which Meta did not release; Maxime Labonne's `mlabonne/open-perfectblend` is the open-source reconstruction that approximates that mixture from public Apache-2.0/MIT datasets.[^1][^2] The HF train split holds exactly **1,420,909 examples** (≈3.04 GB uncompressed, 1.48 GB download), in a `conversations` chat format with a `source` field tagging each sample's origin.[^3] It is deliberately heavy on chat, math, code, and instruction-following — the deduplication pass dropped 88.1k near-duplicates — and pulls from OpenBMB, MetaMath, Hugging Face, Microsoft, theblackcat102, the Post-training-Data-Flywheel, and LMSYS sources. Because Meta's instruction-following and harmful-intent slices were never published, the open version substitutes a different instruction-following source and omits the harmful-intent category entirely.[^1] In the LLM-wiki this dataset matters as the **data-scale
