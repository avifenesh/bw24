--- title: Model-Predictive Path Integral (MPPI) Control type: concept tags: [robotics, control, optimal-control, sampling-based, mpc, gpu, mppi] sources: [] updated: 2026-06-29 --- # Model-Predictive Path Integral (MPPI) Control MPPI is a **sampling-based, derivative-free** algorithm for receding-horizon optimal control: the dominant member of the sampling-based [[model-predictive-control]] family. At each control step it draws `N` candidate control sequences from a Gaussian, `u_t ~ N(μ_t, Σ_t)`, rolls every sequence forward through a (possibly nonlinear, non-smooth) dynamics model, scores each rolled-out trajectory by a cumulative cost `L_n`, and sets the new nominal control to a softmax-weighted average of the samples, `μ_t = Σ ω_n u_{n,t}` with `ω_n ∝ exp(-(L_n - L_min)/λ)`. The temperature `λ` controls how selectively the weighting favors low-cost samples. Because no gradients of the dynamics or cost are ever taken, MPPI handles contact-rich dynamics, discontinuous costs, and black-box simulators that defeat derivative-based nonlinear MPC — at the price of needing many parallel rollouts (often thousands) per control step.
