--- title: Capacity margin type: concept tags: [robotics, manipulability, polytopes, control, metrics] sources: [] updated: 2026-06-29 --- # Capacity margin **Capacity margin** is a scalar feasibility metric used in polytope-based analysis of a robot's physical abilities. Given a *capacity polytope* — the convex set of task-space velocities, accelerations, or forces a manipulator can produce at a given configuration, subject to joint velocity/torque limits, kinematic constraints, and obstacle proximity — the capacity margin is the **signed distance from a required task point (a desired wrench or twist) to the nearest facet of that polytope**. A positive margin means the required task lies inside the feasible set with room to spare; a zero margin means the task sits exactly on a boundary; a negative margin means the task is infeasible at that configuration. Because it is signed and continuous, the margin is differentiable enough to serve directly as a cost or constraint in optimization, unlike binary feasibility checks.[^1][^2] The metric grew out of the constrained-manipulability and force/velocity polytope line of work.
