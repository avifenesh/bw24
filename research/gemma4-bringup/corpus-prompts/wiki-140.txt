--- title: DART block-parallel drafting type: concept tags: [speculative-decoding, drafter, block-parallel, diffusion-llm, eagle3] sources: [] updated: 2026-06-29 --- # DART block-parallel drafting DART (Diffusion-Inspired Speculative Decoding for Fast LLM Inference) is a speculative-decoding drafter design from Fuliang Liu and colleagues (arXiv:2601.19278, submitted 2026-01-27, Nanjing University and collaborators), with code released at github.com/fvliang/DART.[^1] Its premise is that autoregressive drafters such as [[eagle3|EAGLE-3]] raise draft accuracy but pay a sequential cost: even with a single custom layer, EAGLE-3's multi-step rollouts consume roughly 20-40% of total inference time, turning the drafting forward pass itself into a new bottleneck. DART borrows the parallel-generation idea from diffusion-based LLMs (dLLMs) without inheriting their drawbacks. Rather than running a standalone bidirectional dLLM (the paper notes that drop-in dLLM drafters like DiffuSpec/Dream7B are mismatched to the causal, prefix-conditioned setting and are tens of times heavier per step), DART predicts logits for multiple future masked positions in parallel within a single forward pass, conditioned on the target model's hidden states.
