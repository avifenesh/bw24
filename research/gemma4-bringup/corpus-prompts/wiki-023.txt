--- title: Multi-Agent Topology and Reliability type: concept tags: [agents, multi-agent, topology, reliability, verification, safety] sources: [research-2026-06-26-aiml-multi-agent-topology-reliability] updated: 2026-06-29 --- # Multi-Agent Topology and Reliability Multi-agent LLM systems (MAS) fail far more often than raw capability predicts — sometimes 40–85% on real tasks, often losing to a single well-prompted agent — and the decade's instinct ("add more agents, add a critic, use a better model") is mostly wrong. Reliability is an **organizational property** of how agents are wired, what crosses boundaries, and how recovery is grounded. The field converges on a compact design equation, `R ≈ β·ρ(A)/δ`, super-critical when R > 1, where β is per-hop susceptibility, ρ(A) is the spectral radius of the communication graph, and δ is the effective recovery rate. This is Normal Accident Theory quantified. (Caveat: the equation and several supporting items are 2026 preprints — strong but unsettled.) Failures are designed in, not debugged in. The MAST taxonomy (Cemri et al., arXiv:2503.13657, NeurIPS 2025, 1,600+ traces, κ=0.88) splits 14 failure modes into specification & system design **44.2%** (the largest and most tractable), inter-agent misalignment **32.3%**
