--- title: SLA (Sparse-Linear Attention) type: concept tags: [attention, sparse-attention, linear-attention, diffusion, dit, video-generation, kernel, hardware-squeeze] sources: [] updated: 2026-06-29 --- # SLA (Sparse-Linear Attention) SLA (Sparse-Linear Attention) is a trainable attention method from the THU-ML group (Jintao Zhang, Haoxu Wang, et al., with Ion Stoica and Joseph E. Gonzalez among the authors) that fuses *sparse* and *linear* attention to accelerate Diffusion Transformer (DiT) models, targeting the attention-latency bottleneck of long-sequence video generation.[^1][^2] The paper "SLA: Beyond Sparsity in Diffusion Transformers via Fine-Tunable Sparse-Linear Attention" (arXiv:2509.24006) was submitted 2025-09-28 and was the #1 HuggingFace paper of its day.[^2][^3] The core observation: DiT attention weights split into two parts — a small fraction of large weights that are *high rank* (so they need exact, quadratic attention), and a remaining bulk of weights that are *very low rank* (so they can be approximated cheaply by linear attention).
