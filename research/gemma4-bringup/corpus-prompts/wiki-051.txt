Its central trick is to **split an update into a "removal" phase and a "reclamation" phase**, deferring the free until a **grace period** has elapsed — a window guaranteed long enough that every reader who could have held a reference to the old data has finished. The payoff is that **readers pay essentially zero synchronization cost**: in a server-class (non-preemptible) build, `rcu_read_lock()`/`rcu_read_unlock()` compile to nothing — no atomics, no memory barriers, no cache-line bouncing, no spinning, no blocking. Readers are wait-free; all cost moves to the rare write side. This matters because on SMP/NUMA hardware the dominant cost is not computation but communication cache misses, and conventional locks/rwlocks/refcounts force every reader to do an expensive globally-visible atomic that bounces a cache line.
