--- title: EC2 UltraCluster type: entity tags: [aws, ec2, hpc, networking, gpu, efa] sources: [] updated: 2026-06-29 --- # EC2 UltraCluster Amazon EC2 UltraClusters are AWS's supercomputer-class deployments of thousands of accelerated EC2 instances, co-located in a single Availability Zone and wired together with [[efa|Elastic Fabric Adapter]] networking in a petabit-scale, non-blocking fabric.[^1] They give ML, generative-AI, and HPC workloads on-demand, pay-as-you-go access to tightly-coupled distributed compute without the customer building or maintaining a physical cluster.[^1] The instance families deployed in UltraClusters are the GPU `P6e-GB200`, `P6-B200`, `P5en`, `P5e`, `P5`, `P4d` and the Trainium `Trn2`/`Trn1` types.[^1] Beyond compute and network, each UltraCluster exposes [[fsx-lustre|Amazon FSx for Lustre]] shared storage — a managed parallel file system with sub-millisecond latency for streaming massive training datasets.[^1] This is the infrastructure tier above the individual fleet boxes on [[aws]]; it is the same EFA fabric that the smaller `g6e` L40S box ([[l40s-eu-north-box]]) sits on, but scaled out.
