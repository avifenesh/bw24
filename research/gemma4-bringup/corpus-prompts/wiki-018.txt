--- title: Arithmetic Intensity type: concept tags: [performance, gpu, profiling, memory-bound, compute-bound, roofline, hardware-squeeze] sources: [] updated: 2026-06-30 --- # Arithmetic Intensity Arithmetic intensity (also called operational intensity) is the ratio of arithmetic operations performed to bytes of memory traffic moved by a kernel, expressed in FLOP/byte.[^1] It is the single number that places a kernel on the x-axis of the [[roofline-model]], and therefore decides whether the kernel is [[memory-bound-vs-compute-bound|memory-bound or compute-bound]]: below the model's ridge point a kernel cannot supply the compute units fast enough and is capped by bandwidth; above it the kernel is limited by peak FLOP/s.[^1] The metric exists because modern GPUs have a large gap between arithmetic bandwidth and memory bandwidth, so the most efficient kernels are the ones that do many operations per byte loaded — when a kernel is memory-bound, the standard lever is to raise its arithmetic intensity by shifting work from the memory subsystem onto the compute units.[^1] What makes a workload friendly to throughput hardware is how its arithmetic intensity *scales* with problem size, since different algorithms have different operational
