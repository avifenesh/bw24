--- title: TensorDock type: entity tags: [gpu, cloud, marketplace, neocloud, infrastructure, rtx, h100] sources: [] updated: 2026-06-29 --- # TensorDock TensorDock is a GPU cloud marketplace founded by Jonathon Lei that rents accelerated compute for AI training, inference, and rendering workloads. Rather than owning all its hardware, it runs a marketplace model in which independent hosts compete and set their own prices, which keeps per-hour rates low; it bills pay-per-second and lets users start for as little as $5.[^1][^2] The catalog spans a wide hardware range — single-slot NVIDIA RTX cards (down to a modest A4000) up to NVLink'd NVIDIA HGX H100 SXM5 servers and, more recently, RTX Blackwell — and TensorDock was among the first providers to market with true on-demand H100s for under $3/hr.[^3] In January 2026 it launched "Core Cloud," a platform that deploys GPU virtual machines in as little as 45 seconds, advertising GPUs from about $0.29/hr.[^4] TensorDock enters this wiki through its acquisition: on **March 26, 2025** the GPUaaS provider [[voltage-park|Voltage Park]] acquired TensorDock to broaden its on-demand catalog beyond bare-metal HGX H100 into single-slot
