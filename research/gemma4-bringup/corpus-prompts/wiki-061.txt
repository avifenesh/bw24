--- title: HiFloat4 (HiF4) type: concept tags: [quantization, fp4, ascend, npu, kv-cache, ptq, hifloat, formats] sources: [] updated: 2026-06-29 --- # HiFloat4 (HiF4) HiFloat4 (HiF4) is Huawei's 4-bit member of the HiFloat family, a line of tapered-precision floating-point formats designed for Ascend NPUs rather than NVIDIA tensor cores. It is the 4-bit descendant of **HiFloat8 (HiF8)**, the 8-bit format introduced in Huawei's 2024 white paper "Ascend HiFloat8 Format for Deep Learning" (arXiv:2409.16626).[^2] HiF8's signature idea is a **dynamic prefix code** that adapts the split between exponent and mantissa bits to the magnitude of each value: for normal numbers it offers 7 exponent values with a 3-bit mantissa, 8 exponent values with a 2-bit mantissa, and 16 exponent values with a 1-bit mantissa, giving near-FP16 dynamic range (~38 binades) in 8 bits — much wider than the fixed E4M3/E5M2 FP8 layouts.
