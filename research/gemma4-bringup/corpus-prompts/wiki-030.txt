--- title: llguidance type: entity status: active tags: [constrained-decoding, structured-output, json-schema, grammar, rust, inference, serving] sources: [] updated: 2026-06-30 --- # llguidance llguidance (full name "Low-level Guidance") is a Rust library for [[constrained-decoding]] — it enforces an arbitrary context-free grammar on an LLM's token-by-token output, computing at each decode step the token mask of all tokens that could still extend the prefix into a valid string. It is developed under the `guidance-ai` organization (a Microsoft open-source project, governed by the Microsoft CLA) and is the grammar engine behind the Python [[guidance]] library since Guidance v0.2.0 (2025-01-07). The library reached **v1.0.0 on 2025-06-23**.[^1] Its defining performance claim is roughly **50μs of single-core CPU time per token** for a 128k-vocab tokenizer, with negligible startup cost — cheap enough that, with 16 cores and a 10ms forward pass, it can sustain batch sizes up to about 3200 without slowing the model.[^1] Architecturally, llguidance is the canonical **on-the-fly** mask engine, the opposite pole from precompute engines like Outlines and [[xgrammar]].
