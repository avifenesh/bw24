--- title: ENA Express type: concept tags: [aws, networking, ec2, srd, latency, bandwidth] sources: [] updated: 2026-06-29 --- # ENA Express ENA Express is an Amazon EC2 networking feature that layers the AWS **Scalable Reliable Datagram (SRD)** protocol underneath ordinary TCP and UDP traffic between two instances. SRD is the same high-performance transport that powers Elastic Fabric Adapters; it spreads the packets of a single flow across many independent network paths (up to 64 at a time), dynamically reroutes away from congested paths, and handles packet reordering and most retransmits in the Nitro network card so the application layer sees an in-order, reliable stream.[^1][^2] The headline benefits are two: it raises the bandwidth a *single* flow can use from the usual **5 Gbps cap up to 25 Gbps** (still bounded by the instance's aggregate limit), and it cuts tail latency sharply — AWS measured up to **50% lower P99 and up to 85% lower P99.9** latency versus plain TCP during congestion.[^1][^2] It launched November 29, 2022, initially only on `c6gn.16xlarge`, is free of charge, and now spans well over a
