--- title: Triton type: entity status: active tags: [gpu, kernel-dsl, openai, mlir, cuda, rocm, compiler] sources: [] updated: 2026-06-29 --- # Triton Triton is an open-source Python-embedded language and compiler for writing high-performance custom GPU kernels (matmul, attention, fused elementwise) without dropping to CUDA C++. It was created by Philippe Tillet during his PhD at Harvard and first presented in the MAPL 2019 paper *"Triton: An Intermediate Language and Compiler for Tiled Neural Network Computations,"* then re-implemented at OpenAI and released as Triton 1.0 in July 2021 under an MIT license.[^1][^2] The pitch is a productivity/flexibility sweet spot: fast code at higher productivity than raw CUDA but more flexible than vendor-locked DSLs, with the compiler — not the programmer — owning intra-block thread scheduling, memory coalescing, and shared-memory management.[^3] The kernel author works at the granularity of *blocks* (program instances over tiles), which is the same tile-centric abstraction that NVIDIA later baked into hardware and its own [[cuda-tile-ir]] frontend. Triton 2.0 (2023) rebuilt the backend on MLIR, which became the foundation for multi-vendor targeting and for kernels that fuse back-to-back
