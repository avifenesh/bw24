--- title: Supply-chain pinning type: concept tags: [security, supply-chain, ci, github-actions, dependencies, integrity-verification] sources: [] updated: 2026-06-29 --- # Supply-chain pinning Supply-chain pinning is the practice of referencing every external dependency by an **immutable identifier** — most often a full Git commit SHA, plus a content hash where one is available — instead of a mutable name like a release tag, a `^`/`~` semver range, or a default branch. The threat it closes is the **tag-repointing attack**: a tag such as `v4` or a branch such as `main` is just a movable pointer, so anyone who compromises the upstream repo (or a maintainer account) can retroactively move that tag to malicious code, and every downstream consumer pulls the new payload on their next install or CI run without changing a line of their own config. A 40-character commit SHA, by contrast, can never be made to point at different code, so a pin is a one-time trust decision that holds until you deliberately bump it.
