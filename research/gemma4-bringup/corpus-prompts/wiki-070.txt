--- title: SOAR (Scale Optimization for Accurate Reconstruction) type: concept tags: [quantization, nvfp4, fp4, ptq, scale-selection, blackwell] sources: [] updated: 2026-06-29 --- # SOAR (Scale Optimization for Accurate Reconstruction) SOAR (Scale Optimization for Accurate Reconstruction, Shanghai Jiao Tong University + Tencent Hunyuan, arXiv:2605.12245, May 2026) is a post-training quantization (PTQ) framework that improves [[nvfp4|NVFP4]] accuracy by attacking the **scale-selection** axis rather than the FP4 grid. The paper's premise is that existing NVFP4 methods leave accuracy on the table for two reasons: scale selection is inflexible (typically a single absmax-derived scale per block), and the quantization scale and dequantization scale are treated as one coupled value. SOAR has two components. **Closed-form Joint Scale Optimization (CJSO)** jointly optimizes the global (per-tensor) and block-wise (per-16-element) scales using analytical solutions derived from minimizing reconstruction error, rather than a fixed absmax rule. **Decoupled Scale Search (DSS)** then separates the high-precision quantization scale from its lower-precision (FP8-E4M3-constrained) dequantization counterpart and runs a discrete search to recover precision lost when the scale itself is quantized.[^1] The optimization is calibration-data-free and cheap — the referencing analysis notes roughly
