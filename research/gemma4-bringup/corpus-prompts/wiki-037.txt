--- title: MoE Routing Surgery & Expert Compression type: concept tags: [moe, expert-pruning, expert-merge, model-compression, routing, healing, quantization] sources: [shared-delta-moe-project, gemma-expert-atlas] updated: 2026-06-29 --- # MoE Routing Surgery & Expert Compression This is the central concept for compressing Mixture-of-Experts models by acting on their experts and router rather than on raw bit-width. It pulls together a long empirical campaign across [[shared-delta-moe]] (OLMoE-1B-7B, Qwen3-30B-A3B) and [[gemma-expert-atlas]] (gemma-4-26B-A4B). The arc is: try to share/merge redundant experts, discover that the redundancy needed for free GAIN is not where it was assumed to be, and end on pruning low-saliency experts plus healing as the one approach that actually scales. The single most important finding is that **expert redundancy is ACTIVATION/FUNCTION-defined, not WEIGHT-defined**. On OLMoE, experts share input directions (CKA 0.51, subspace ratio 2.7x) yet their raw matrices are nearly orthogonal (template axis k90 = 57/64, mean_energy = 2%). The reconciliation: two experts can act alike on real data while being orthogonal in weight space. This kills additive-mean-base schemes — D2-MoE's "subtract a shared mean" is structurally wrong here because the mean carries only ~2%
