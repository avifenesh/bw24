--- title: Scaling Laws for Precision type: concept tags: [scaling-laws, quantization, low-precision, precision-aware, post-training-quantization, effective-parameter-count, compute-optimal] sources: [] updated: 2026-06-29 --- # Scaling Laws for Precision "Scaling Laws for Precision" is the title of a November 2024 paper (Kumar, Ankner, Spector, Bordelon, Muennighoff, Paul, Pehlevan, Ré, Raghunathan; arXiv:2411.04330, submitted 7 Nov 2024, last revised 30 Nov 2024) that extends the standard Chinchilla-style loss-vs-parameters-vs-tokens scaling laws to account for numeric precision in both training and inference.[^1] Existing scaling laws assume a fixed datatype and say nothing about what happens when weights or activations are pushed to low bit-widths, yet precision directly changes both the quality and the cost of a model.
