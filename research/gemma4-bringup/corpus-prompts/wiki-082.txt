The universal contract: a reader must reserve-then-publish a pointer before dereferencing; a reclaimer must scan all published reservations and only free what nobody reserved. Every scheme is a different bet on how coarse the reservation is and where the synchronization cost lands. ## Key findings - Three classic families: Hazard Pointers (per-pointer reservation, robust, but a full store->load seq_cst fence on every node hop - ~50% of CPU on a 100-node Harris-Michael list with 128 threads; hard memory bound H+R*N); Epoch-Based Reclamation (per-operation epoch, ~free reads, but NOT robust - one stalled thread pins the epoch forever ->
