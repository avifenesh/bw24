--- title: Goedel-mHC (Gödel Machines) type: entity status: active tags: [model, architecture, hyper-connections, residual-connections, nanogpt-speedrun, open-model] sources: [] updated: 2026-06-29 --- # Goedel-mHC (Gödel Machines) **Goedel-mHC** is the first open 1B+ language model to use **Manifold-Constrained Hyper-Connections (mHC)** as its residual-connection mechanism, released as the `GoedelMachines/Goedel-mHC-1B` checkpoint on HuggingFace by the Gödel Machines group.[^1] It is explicitly an *architecture research release* rather than a usable assistant: a 1,009M-parameter base model trained on just 20B tokens of FineWeb-Edu to validate that mHC, combined with modern attention and FFN tricks from the NanoGPT speedrun community, scales better than a standard transformer at equal compute. The headline result is **3.8% better bits-per-byte with 15% fewer parameters** than a GQA + SwiGLU + PreNorm + AdamW baseline trained identically on the same data.[^1] This is the earliest public model to cite the DeepSeek [[manifold-constrained-hyper-connections|mHC paper]] (arXiv:2512.24880), and is the open line referenced from that page.[^1] The architecture stacks four ideas.
