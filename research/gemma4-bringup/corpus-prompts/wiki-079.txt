--- title: AMD Instinct MI355X type: entity status: active tags: [hardware, gpu, amd, accelerator, cdna] sources: [] updated: 2026-06-29 --- The **AMD Instinct MI355X** is AMD's MI350-series datacenter AI/HPC accelerator, the first generation built on the 4th-gen CDNA 4 architecture and AMD's direct answer to NVIDIA's Blackwell B200/B300. Each module packages eight CDNA 4 compute dies linked over 4th-gen Infinity Fabric (185 billion transistors total), surrounded by ten HBM3E stacks delivering **288 GB of on-package memory at 8 TB/s bandwidth** plus a 64 MB Infinity Cache (L3).[^1][^2] The headline addition over CDNA 3 (MI300X/MI325X) is native low-precision matrix-core support for **MXFP4 and MXFP6** alongside FP8, roughly doubling dense AI throughput per clock and giving AMD a quoted peak around 20 PFLOPS in FP4.[^1][^2] Memory grows 50% over the 192 GB MI300X and bandwidth 51% (5.3→8 TB/s), so models in the hundreds of billions of parameters can stay resident on a single GPU with fewer shards.
