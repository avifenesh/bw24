--- title: Audio Tokenization & Full-Duplex Speech LLMs type: concept tags: [audio, speech-llm, full-duplex, rvq, tokenization, streaming, multimodal] sources: [research-2026-06-28-aiml-audio-tokenization-full-duplex] updated: 2026-06-29 --- # Audio Tokenization & Full-Duplex Speech LLMs The core thesis: in a modern speech LLM **the tokenizer is not an afterthought, it is the architecture.** Token design directly determines bitrate, latency, semantic richness, acoustic fidelity, streaming feasibility, full-duplex capability, and downstream LM modeling difficulty. This page covers the architecture and full-duplex frontier; the codec-design fundamentals are in [[speech-codec-tokenization]] and the cross-modal sync layer in [[omni-modal-alignment]]. It touches [[latent-bridge]] (the owner's closed-latent-loop reasoner work). The field has converged on a brutal three-way tension: low enough bitrate for real-time AR modeling, rich enough semantics for linguistic reasoning, faithful enough acoustics for natural prosody. **Semantic tokens** (HuBERT/WavLM + k-means) capture phonetic/linguistic content but discard speaker/prosody; **acoustic tokens** (EnCodec/DAC RVQ) preserve fidelity but spread information across many LM-unfriendly layers (RVQ energy concentrates in layer 1; later layers model diminishing residuals).
