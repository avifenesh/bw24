--- title: Inception Labs type: entity status: active tags: [diffusion-llm, mercury, startup, palo-alto, stefano-ermon] sources: [] updated: 2026-06-29 --- # Inception Labs Inception Labs (publicly branded just "Inception") is a Palo Alto, California startup founded in 2024 that builds commercial diffusion-based large language models — the producer of the [[mercury-coder|Mercury]] family referenced in [[diffusion-llm]]. The founding team is three professors who worked on the core technologies behind modern generative AI: Stanford's Stefano Ermon (CEO), who co-invented score-based diffusion models used in image generation; UCLA's Aditya Grover; and Cornell's Volodymyr Kuleshov.[^1][^2] Ermon's pitch is that diffusion's holistic, parallel, iterative-refinement generation conserves the two metrics that matter most for serving — latency and compute cost — by processing many tokens simultaneously instead of one-at-a-time as autoregressive models like GPT-5 and Gemini do.[^2] The company's headline product is **Mercury**, launched in 2025 as the first commercial-scale diffusion LLM, with a code-focused variant (Mercury Coder) that Inception benchmarks at over 1,000 tokens per second — Inception cites independent Artificial Analysis evals of roughly 1109 tok/s (Mini) and 737 tok/s (Small) on H100s, up to
