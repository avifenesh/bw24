--- title: Learned Hybrid Layer Placement type: concept tags: [ml-architecture, hybrid, linear-attention, neural-architecture-search, ssm, efficiency] sources: [] updated: 2026-06-29 --- # Learned Hybrid Layer Placement In a hybrid model that mixes full (quadratic) attention with cheaper layers — linear attention, sliding-window attention, or SSM blocks — the dominant design question is *which* layers should stay full attention and how many are needed. Most production hybrids answer it by hand: fix a ratio and a pattern (e.g. Qwen3-Next's 3:1 linear:full interleave, Jamba/Nemotron-H's ~7-25% attention sprinkled through Mamba bulk) chosen by intuition and small ablations. **Learned hybrid layer placement** replaces that hand-picking with a trained search: give every block a choice of layer type, let gradients flow through all the alternatives, and let an automated procedure pick the per-layer assignment that maximizes accuracy under a compute or latency budget. This matters because the few full-attention layers are what restore exact recall, induction heads, and in-context learning that the cheap layers lose, so misplacing them is exactly where converted-to-hybrid models break — the same failure MiniMax cited when reverting M2 to full attention
