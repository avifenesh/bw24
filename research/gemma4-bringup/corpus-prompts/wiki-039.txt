--- title: Gated DeltaNet type: concept tags: [linear-attention, delta-rule, gating, hybrid-architecture, long-context, subquadratic, ml-architecture] sources: [qwen36-27b-server-config] updated: 2026-06-29 --- # Gated DeltaNet Gated DeltaNet (GDN) is a linear-attention layer that fuses two complementary fixes to the lossy-associative-memory problem of plain [[linear-attention]]: data-dependent **gating** (a forget gate that decays the recurrent state) and the **delta rule** (an erase-then-write update that cleanly overwrites a stored association). It was introduced by Songlin Yang, Jan Kautz, and Ali Hatamizadeh (MIT CSAIL + NVIDIA) in "Gated Delta Networks: Improving Mamba2 with Delta Rule" (arXiv 2412.06464, Dec 2024; ICLR 2025).[^1] The motivation is that the two existing levers are individually incomplete. Mamba2's gated update `S_t = α_t S_{t-1} + v_t k_t^T` adaptively *forgets* but cannot *target* a specific memory slot for overwrite; DeltaNet's delta update `S_t = S_{t-1} - β_t (S_{t-1} k_t - v_t) k_t^T` — exactly one SGD step on the regression loss `½‖S k_t − v_t‖²`, equivalent to test-time training with batch size 1 — *targets* precise overwrites but lacks a global decay to flush stale context.[^1] GDN combines them into a single recurrence
