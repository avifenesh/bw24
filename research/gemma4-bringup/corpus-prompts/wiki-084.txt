--- title: libmtmd type: concept tags: [llama-cpp, multimodal, vision, audio, ggml, gguf, mmproj, clip] sources: [] updated: 2026-06-29 --- # libmtmd `libmtmd` is the multimodal library inside [[llama-cpp]] — the high-level extension that handles non-textual input (image, audio, and video) on top of the core `libllama` text-inference engine.[^1][^2] Introduced in 2025 to replace the older standalone `llava.cpp` implementation, it unifies all media handling behind one library so the project's tools can share a single multimodal path instead of maintaining per-model glue.[^2] It is consumed by three front-ends: `llama-cli`, `llama-server` (via the OpenAI-compatible `/chat/completions` API), and `llama-mtmd-cli` (a dedicated binary for testing and development).[^1] Functionally, `libmtmd` bridges specialized media encoders to the language model. Each multimodal model needs a paired **multimodal projector** — an `mmproj` GGUF file — that encodes raw media into latent embeddings and projects them into the model's token-embedding space; `libmtmd` loads it, runs the encoder, and splices the resulting embeddings into the text KV cache.[^1][^2] The underlying CLIP encoder (`clip.cpp`) handles GGUF loading and graph building for both vision and audio paths, keyed off metadata fields such
