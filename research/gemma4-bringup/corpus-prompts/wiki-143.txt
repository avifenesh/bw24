--- title: Blackwell SM120 Family (consumer/workstation Blackwell, compute 12.0) type: entity tags: [hardware, nvidia, blackwell, cuda, sm120] sources: [hardware-local-rig] updated: 2026-06-29 --- # Blackwell SM120 Family (consumer/workstation Blackwell, compute 12.0) **SM120** is NVIDIA's CUDA compute-capability-12.0 branch of the Blackwell generation — the **consumer and workstation** silicon, built on the **GB202** die. It covers the GeForce **RTX 5090** and **RTX 5080**, plus both editions (Server and Workstation) of the **RTX PRO 6000 Blackwell**; all share the same GB202 silicon and the same `sm_120` target, differing only in cooling, power, and memory capacity.[^1] It is a distinct compilation target from **SM100** (compute 10.0), the datacenter Blackwell branch (GB100/GB200/GB300 → B100/B200/B300, SXM/superchip). Despite both being "Blackwell," `sm_120` cubins are **not** compatible with `sm_100` cubins — they are separate targets, and CUDA 12.8 was the first toolkit to add `sm_120` (alongside `sm_100`/`sm_101`).[^1][^2] Building for the consumer parts uses `arch=compute_120,code=sm_120`, with the accelerated-feature variant written `sm_120a` (not forward-compatible).[^2] The user's [[hardware-local-rig]] (laptop RTX 5090) sits on this branch, which is why it needs cu128 builds.
